# Lone Working Policy

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this policy is to ensure the health, safety, and welfare of all individuals working for [Business Name] who are required to work alone. "Lone working" refers to situations where staff or contractors work without close or direct supervision.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to the Sole Trader and any contractors, freelancers, or employees working from home, in storage facilities, or traveling for business purposes (e.g., attending auctions, post office runs).

## 3. Risks
Identified risks associated with lone working in this business include:
*   Accidents or sudden illness in the home office or storage area.
*   Manual handling injuries (lifting heavy framed items or boxes) with no assistance available.
*   Security risks when transporting valuable stock to/from post office or events.
*   Isolation and mental well-being.

## 4. Procedures
*   **Check-in System**: Contractors must agree on a regular check-in schedule (e.g., a message at the start and end of the work day) with the business owner.
*   **Safe Handling**: Heavy items (large framed shirts/displays) should not be moved alone if they exceed safe lifting limits.
*   **Home Safety**: The workspace must be kept free of trip hazards. A basic first aid kit should be accessible.
*   **Security**: When carrying high-value stock, individuals should not advertise their contents and should park in well-lit, secure areas.
*   **Emergency Contacts**: A list of emergency contacts must be kept on the phone and in the workspace.

## 5. Review
This policy will be reviewed annually or following any incident involving a lone worker.
